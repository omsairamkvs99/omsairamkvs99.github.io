Who I Am

Artifact 01 A NLP - Chatbot

Medical Chatbot: AI-Powered Health Assistant

Objective To develop a conversational AI system that provides reliable, accessible medical guidance by leveraging NLP techniques.
Process Designed intent recognition pipelines, curated a medical FAQ dataset, built and trained a sequence-to-sequence dialogue model, and iterated based on user testing and accuracy metrics.
Tools Python, NLTK, TensorFlow / Keras, Flask, JSON intent dataset
Value Prop Demonstrates ability to design end-to-end AI solutions, from data preprocessing to deployment, while addressing a meaningful real-world problem in healthcare.
Unique Value Combines NLP knowledge with domain-specific application, showing adaptability across fields, a skill directly transferable to security chatbot and threat-analysis tools.
Relevance NLP and conversational AI are foundational to modern threat intelligence platforms, automated incident response, and SOC tools.
Artifact 01 B Computer Vision - CNN

Facial Expression Recognition Using CNNs

Objective To build a deep learning model capable of classifying human facial expressions in real-time using Convolutional Neural Networks.
Process Collected and preprocessed labeled image datasets, designed a multi-layer CNN architecture, trained with data augmentation techniques, and evaluated performance using confusion matrices and accuracy/loss curves.
Tools Python, TensorFlow, Keras, OpenCV, NumPy, Matplotlib
Value Prop Illustrates hands-on expertise in designing, training, and evaluating deep learning models, a core competency for any AI/ML practitioner working with real-world data.
Unique Value Computer vision skills are directly applicable to biometric security, surveillance anomaly detection, and identity verification systems in cybersecurity.
Relevance CNNs underpin modern AI-driven security systems including facial recognition for access control and video-based threat detection in real-time environments.
Artifact 02 Generative AI - Chatbot

MindCheck: AI-Powered Mental Health Check-In Assistant

Artifact View Chatbot
Objective To design and deploy a motivational AI chatbot that provides college students with a low-barrier, judgment-free space to check in on their emotional wellbeing and receive evidence-based coping strategies in real time.
Process Applied the Design Thinking framework across five phases: conducted user empathy research to identify pain points among college students, defined the problem scope and ethical boundaries, ideated conversation flows and coping strategy content, prototyped the bot using a structured system prompt, and iteratively tested across five behavioral scenarios including a crisis referral protocol.
Tools Chatbase.co, Prompt Engineering, Claude AI, Design Thinking Framework
Value Prop Demonstrates practical fluency in prompt engineering, responsible AI design, and human-centered product thinking — the intersection of technical and ethical competencies that defines effective AI/ML practitioners in real-world deployment contexts.
Unique Value Bridges AI capability with social impact by addressing a documented mental health access gap in higher education, showcasing the ability to scope, build, and constrain AI systems for sensitive, user-facing applications.
Relevance Conversational AI and LLM-powered assistants are among the fastest-growing deployment areas across healthcare, education, and enterprise software — this artifact reflects direct hands-on experience building within that paradigm responsibly.
Artifact 03 Agentic AI - Business Intelligence

The Lobster Report: How OpenClaw Triggered a Big Tech Arms Race

Artifact View Newsletter
Objective To research, analyze, and communicate the rise of OpenClaw as an agentic AI platform and examine how five major technology companies (Tencent, Alibaba, ByteDance, Baidu, and Nvidia) strategically responded, providing business decision-makers with actionable intelligence on the agentic AI shift.
Process Gathered and synthesized primary reporting from Fortune, CNBC, Reuters, and 36Kr; mapped each company's response timeline, product strategy, and competitive logic; structured findings into a professional newsletter format with data visualizations, company deep dives, a commercial impact analysis, a risk assessment, and a forward-looking deployment forecast.
Tools Industry research sources (Fortune, CNBC, Reuters, 36Kr), AI trend analysis, newsletter design, business intelligence frameworks
Value Prop Demonstrates the ability to translate fast-moving AI developments into structured, audience-ready intelligence, a critical skill for AI/ML practitioners who must communicate complex technical shifts to business stakeholders, leadership teams, and cross-functional collaborators.
Unique Value Goes beyond surface-level trend reporting by analyzing the strategic logic behind each company's approach, covering governance risks including 512 documented security vulnerabilities and prompt injection attacks, and contextualizing agentic AI as infrastructure rather than just a product category.
Relevance Agentic AI systems that act autonomously represent the next frontier for both productivity and security risk. Understanding how these platforms scale, where their vulnerabilities lie, and how enterprises are adopting them is directly relevant to building AI-powered cybersecurity strategies and threat response systems.
Artifact 04 AI History - Interactive Timeline

AI Timeline (1956 - 2024): Failures, Controversies and Breakthroughs

Artifact View Timeline
Objective To document the complete arc of artificial intelligence from its formal origin at the 1956 Dartmouth Conference through to the 2024 Nobel Prize, with an honest emphasis on the failures and controversies that mainstream AI narratives tend to overlook.
Process Researched key milestones across nearly seven decades of AI history, curated events that represented not just technical progress but also ethical controversies and high-profile failures, then structured and coded the entire timeline as a standalone HTML file with clean visual formatting for easy navigation.
Tools HTML, CSS, historical research across academic and industry sources, GitHub Pages for deployment
Value Prop Demonstrates the ability to synthesize decades of complex technical history into a clear, accessible, and web-ready format, showing both research depth and practical front-end implementation skills that are directly applicable to knowledge communication in AI/ML roles.
Unique Value Rather than only celebrating AI milestones, this timeline deliberately surfaces the failures and controversies that shaped the field, offering a more complete and critical perspective on how AI has evolved, which is essential context for anyone building responsible AI systems.
Relevance Understanding AI's history of failures and ethical controversies is foundational to building safer systems today, particularly in high-stakes fields like cybersecurity where past mistakes in autonomous decision-making carry direct lessons for modern threat detection and AI governance.
