1950s–60s · Dawn
breakthrough
1956

Dartmouth conference coins "AI"

McCarthy, Minsky, Shannon and Simon gather over summer and birth the field. The ambition is total: machines that think, learn, create. Human-level AI feels decades — not centuries — away.
▾
breakthrough
1958

Perceptron invented

Frank Rosenblatt builds a machine that classifies images using weighted connections. The press declares it the embryo of an electronic brain. Neural networks are born.
▾
crash
1969

Minsky kills the perceptron

Minsky & Papert's book proves perceptrons cannot solve XOR. Neural network funding collapses almost overnight. The first AI winter begins.
▾
1970s–80s · Expert Systems
breakthrough
1972

MYCIN diagnoses infections

Stanford's rule-based system matches specialist physicians on blood infection diagnosis. Proof that AI can beat humans in a narrow, high-stakes domain — the first real clinical AI.
▾
crash
1973

Lighthill report kills AI funding

UK mathematician James Lighthill's government review calls AI research a broad failure. DARPA slashes budgets. A second AI winter spreads from Britain across the Atlantic.
▾
breakthrough
1981

Japan's Fifth Generation project

Japan commits ¥100B to build intelligent computers. The US and UK counter-fund AI in a panic, inadvertently re-energising the global field for the next wave of progress.
▾
crash
1987

Lisp Machine market collapses

Specialised AI hardware companies fold overnight as general-purpose PCs outpace them. Hundreds of millions evaporate. The third AI winter begins and expert-system companies start to unravel.
▾
crash
1989

Expert systems go bust

Companies betting on expert-system shells lose over $1.5B as the technology proves too brittle to maintain. Japan's Fifth Generation project quietly shutters. The corporate AI bubble bursts completely.
▾
1990s · Statistical Turn
breakthrough
1997

Deep Blue defeats Kasparov

IBM's chess computer beats the world champion in a six-game match under tournament conditions. The first time a machine defeats a reigning world champion in a complex strategy game.
▾
crash
1999

Early autonomous vehicles fail

Multiple high-profile DARPA demo failures reveal self-driving in real conditions is vastly harder than controlled tests suggest. Optimism proves wildly premature, stalling billions in investment for years.
▾
2000s · Data & Scale
breakthrough
2011

Watson wins Jeopardy!

IBM's Watson defeats champions Ken Jennings and Brad Rutter — demonstrating natural language understanding at a level that stuns the public and signals a new era of AI confidence in unstructured data.
▾
crash
2009–13

Google Flu Trends crashes

Google's flu-prediction model overestimates prevalence by 2×. A landmark case study in overfitting, spurious correlation, and the fragility of AI systems built on proxy signals.
▾
breakthrough
2012

AlexNet ignites deep learning

Hinton's team wins ImageNet by a 10-point margin using a GPU-trained deep CNN. Every major lab pivots to deep learning within 18 months. The modern AI era effectively begins here.
▾
2016–20 · Deep Learning Era
breakthrough
2016

AlphaGo defeats Lee Sedol

DeepMind beats the 18-time world Go champion 4–1. Move 37 in game 2 is called a "divine move" — no human would have played it. Go, thought to be 20 years away, falls almost immediately.
▾
controversy
2016

Microsoft Tay turned racist in 24h

Tay, a Twitter chatbot designed to learn from users, was deliberately manipulated into posting offensive content within a single day. A defining warning about adversarial misuse of open-ended AI systems.
▾
crash
2018

Uber self-driving kills pedestrian

Elaine Herzberg is struck and killed by an Uber autonomous vehicle in Arizona — the first pedestrian fatality by a self-driving car. The safety driver was distracted. Regulation debates intensify globally.
▾
breakthrough
2020

GPT-3 shocks the world

OpenAI releases GPT-3 with 175B parameters. It writes poetry, code, essays and dialogue at a quality that blurs the line between human and machine. The foundation-model era begins.
▾
2021–24 · Foundation Models
breakthrough
2021

AlphaFold solves protein folding

DeepMind predicts protein structures at near-experimental accuracy — cracking a 50-year grand challenge in biology. 200M+ predicted structures released free to scientists, accelerating drug discovery by years.
▾
breakthrough
2022

ChatGPT reaches 100M users in 60 days

The fastest consumer product adoption in recorded history. OpenAI's ChatGPT makes conversational AI mainstream, triggering a global race among Google, Microsoft, Meta and dozens of new entrants.
▾
controversy
2022

Facial recognition drives wrongful arrests

Multiple US cities report wrongful arrests driven by facial recognition errors — disproportionately affecting Black men. Congressional hearings follow. Several cities ban the technology outright.
▾
crash
2023

LLMs hallucinate in court — lawyers sanctioned

Attorneys cite ChatGPT-invented case law in federal court submissions. Judges sanction the attorneys. Hallucination becomes the defining liability challenge for deploying AI in professional contexts.
▾
breakthrough
2024

Hinton & Hopfield win Nobel Prize in Physics

The Nobel Committee awards the Physics prize to the fathers of deep learning — cementing AI as one of the defining scientific achievements of the century, vindicating decades dismissed during the AI winters.
▾
controversy
2024

AI copyright wars reach the courts

Getty Images, artists' coalitions and major newspapers sue AI companies over training data. Courts begin drawing lines on infringement. The IP framework of the internet faces its deepest challenge since Napster.
▾
